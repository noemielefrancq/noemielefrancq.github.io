I am a PhD student at the University of Cambridge working with Prof. Henrik Salje at the Department of Genetics (Pathogen Dynamics Group) and Prof. Julian Parkhill at the Department of Veterinary Medicine (Pathogen Genetics and Evolution Group).

I work on the development of mathematical models that help shed light on the underlying transmission dynamics of infectious diseases, with the ultimate goal of helping disease control. I am particularly focusing on the use of genetic data, that is increasingly available and is bringing novel insights into pathogen transmission.

I am working with datasets of Bordetella pertussis genomes from throughout France as well as other European countries in a collaborative project with Prof. Sylvain Brisse at Institut Pasteur Paris and the national reference center for Pertussis in France. I also work with Listeria monocytogenes, Streptococcus pneumoniae, SARS-CoV-2 and dengue virus.
